A fine morning. Wind NW. Received a letter from Messrs Hurry & Co & Stephenson of the ship having struck upon the bar in going into Yarmouth. Wrote to them to deliver & sell the coals, get the leak stopped & return to Shields. Wrote also to WBS. Went across the water & saw Mr Potts who is to send me a copy of the Harmongs protest on Monday. Tom Smith & I dined at home at 5 o'clock & had the whist club in the evening. A pleasant party. Jobling returned late
