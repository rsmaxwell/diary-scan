We were disturbed again this morning at about 3 o'clock by a heavy squall with rain from the SSW which increased. I & Nicholson immediately turned out, the ship being off the Tees by the time we got upon deck, they had got the top sail & missen stowed & top sail reefed, lay the ship too soon afterwards left with the head to the land, between Seaham & Hartlepool, the squall gradually subsided & at day light 5 o'clock fine weather again. Ran the ship in for the land & between 7 & 8 o'clock got a piolet on board. Lay off the harbour until the tide flowed sufficiently for us to run the bar, which we did at 11 o'clock, working up to the narrows & then dropping up to the Lenickeln shore where I landed & came home. Robert got my things on shore.

I dined with J Tinley & in the evening called & saw Bess, got home before 11 o'clock
