Left Dublin outside the Carlow coach at 9 o'clock AM. When I arrived at 4 o'clock in a state of anxiety impossible for me to describe, misery was in store for me & on enquiring of the servant "how my sister was?" to my insufferable horror I learned she was dead. The fever was so malignant nature could not struggle with it & and on Sunday last at 10 o'clock I lost one of the kindest & most affectionate of sisters & the town one of the best & most benevolent of human beings. I was doomed not even one last parting look at her cold remains for such was the rapidity with which decomposition took place that her remains were obliged to be deposited in the select tomb next to her dear husband yesterday.
