A pleasant morning but rather cloudy. Wind NE. F Barclay & I got up early & bathed. Started after breakfast & travelled along undercliff & saw the difficult gentlemans seats. Most beautiful & romantic well wooded & cliffs very grand. Stopped at Blackgang Chine, immense high cliffs with waterfall, continued our journey through a beautiful & well cultivated country to Freshwater Bay, where we had lunch about 4 o'clock. Captain Lewis & the ladies went by land to see the Needles. F Barclay, Bob Marsh, WBS and self & two servants went by water & got very well to the Needles which with the cave & majestic chalk cliffs, were very grand. But our return was attended with much unpleasantness to say the least, having an ebb tide & strong wind against us & being in a bad and cranky sea boat, we all got most uncomfortably wet by the spray of the sea. With much difficulty got back to Freshwater Bay to an 8 o'clock dinner. This is a very retired & pretty place
