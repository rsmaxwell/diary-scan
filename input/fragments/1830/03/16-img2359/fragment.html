A fine morning. Wind NW & rather cold. Tom Smith, his mother & Fanny left us for Foyston & fortunately without any more unpleasantness. After they left us the weather changed & we had a very stormy day, heavy showers of snow Was & I had the servants up & told them of our plans. Robert agreed to remain with me, also Leadbetter. Hutchison of course leaves us, but will stay in the house until she gets a situation or I wish her to leave. Stephenson has been at NCastle but could obtain nothing for the Hope only 8/6 pecght offering. Ordered Him to unbind the sails. Gave him a draft upon Chapman for £20 to pay wages. At home all the evening
