Fine weather but still a fresh westerly breeze, which detains us. Relieved from pain by the flannel & hartshorn, but my face still much swollen. Remained on board & upon the mole head all the morning.

Mr Bookers clerk called to demand 15 rubbles brokerage for the copper. Went on shore at 5 o'clock to see him upon the subject. It appears I treated with a broker & nob the merchant or I supposed, the brokerage therefore due. Desired Mr Booker to request Mr Hubbardth to pay it & I should settle it in London, having no Russian money left. Amused ourselves on the mole head all evening
