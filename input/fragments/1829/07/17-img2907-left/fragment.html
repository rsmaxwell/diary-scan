... arrived before 12 o'clock. Had to take our luggage again to the Customs House to be searched. A mere matter of form, for they only opened the boxes without turning the things over & after a little trouble & delay, we were allowed to carry all on board, accompanied by an officer. Employed ourselves during this afternoon in stowing our traps away & putting everything in order.

After tea the captain, Marsh & I went on shore, called at the butchers & baker & spent the remainder of the evening at a friend's where we had some wine. Got on board again between 11 & 12 o'clock.

Taken 250 casks of tallow on board
