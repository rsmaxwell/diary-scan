After a regular good & sound snooze, turned out at ½ past 7 o'clock.

During the night slight shower with very little wind. To our great joy when we looked up through the cabin sky light, we saw the yards square & studding sails set. Wind WNW. After breakfast & commenced a heavy rain, which continued until 12 o'clock. Saw the Shaw light at 2 o'clock.

Employed myself in painting the ship's name upon the skiff & the carpenter finishing painting the underside. Bob Marsh being too lazy to complete his work.

Wind continuing favourable & a fine fresh breeze going 5 or 6 knots. At 9 o'clock saw the Trindlew & the island of Lcefsoe bearing NW by W, distant 5 or 6 miles.

Took our warm glass of brandy & water & got to bed before 10 o'clock. Looking the sea evidently rising.
